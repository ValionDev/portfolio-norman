Case Study

A-SCEND

Leading product design for A-LIGN's compliance and audit platform. Making complex, high-stakes audit workflows clear and efficient for 29,000+ users.

Company

Overview

A-SCEND is A-LIGN's flagship compliance and audit management platform. It serves over 5,000 customer organizations and 29,000+ active users: a mix of internal auditors and external clients navigating complex regulatory workflows.

As lead product designer, I own the design process end to end: from discovery and user research through wireframes, high-fidelity UI, design system components, and interactive prototypes built in Figma and Claude Code.

The Challenge

Audit and compliance work is inherently complex. Users deal with dense regulatory requirements, multi-step workflows, and high-stakes data where errors have real consequences. The platform needed to serve two very different audiences:

  • Expert auditors who need speed, density, and efficiency. They don't want oversimplified interfaces that slow them down
  • External clients who may interact with audit processes infrequently and need clear guidance through unfamiliar workflows

Balancing these needs, making dense information scannable without dumbing it down, is the core design tension.

Approach

My process centers on reducing ambiguity early. Rather than handing off static mockups and hoping they translate, I build interactive prototypes that stakeholders and engineers can actually click through:

  • Discovery & research: Understanding auditor workflows, pain points, and mental models through stakeholder interviews and data analysis (PostHog, FullStory)
  • User flows & wireframes: Mapping the information architecture before any visual design, testing structure assumptions early
  • Interactive prototyping: Building working prototypes in Figma and Claude Code that simulate real data and workflows, not just happy paths
  • Design system evolution: Building and evolving reusable components, patterns, and foundations to keep a fast-growing product consistent
  • Cross-functional delivery: Partnering with product, engineering, and marketing from concept through QA

Key Design Decisions

Analytics & Reporting

Designed analytics views that make dense compliance data scannable and actionable. The challenge: auditors need to see everything at a glance, while managers need high-level summaries. The solution uses progressive disclosure: summary metrics up top, with drill-down capabilities that don't require page navigation.

Complex Workflow Simplification

Audit workflows can span weeks with dozens of stakeholders. I mapped these end-to-end and designed clear status indicators, smart defaults, and contextual guidance that reduce cognitive load without hiding necessary complexity.

AI-Assisted Features

Designed AI-powered features within the platform that help auditors work more efficiently. Specifics are under NDA, but the design approach focused on keeping the human in the loop. AI as an accelerator, not a replacement for professional judgment.

Impact

  • Platform serves 29,000+ active users across 5,000+ customer organizations
  • End-to-end design ownership from discovery through production
  • Promoted from UX Designer to Senior UX Designer based on impact
  • Built and evolved design system foundations used across the product
  • Interactive prototyping approach reduced misalignment between design intent and implementation

Reflection

Designing for expert users in high-stakes domains requires a different mindset than consumer product design. The goal isn't simplification for its own sake. It's making complex information clear enough to act on without removing the nuance that professionals need. That tension shapes every design decision.

Working with product analytics tools (PostHog, FullStory) fundamentally changed how I approach design. Seeing actual user behavior, not just what people say in interviews, grounds decisions in reality.
