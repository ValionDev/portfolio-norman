Animation and motion design work across branding, product interfaces, and creative projects, bringing static designs to life with purposeful movement.

Overview

Motion design is a core part of my creative toolkit. At RednBlue, I delivered motion graphics across the company's digital channels, from branded animations to interface micro-interactions. This work spans tools like Rive, After Effects, and Lottie, covering both broadcast-style motion graphics and in-product UI animation.

Motion in Product Design

Motion isn't decoration, it's communication. In product interfaces, well-crafted animations:

  • Guide attention: drawing focus to state changes, new content, or important actions
  • Provide feedback: confirming user actions with responsive, tactile interactions
  • Reduce cognitive load: smoothing transitions between states so users maintain context
  • Add personality: giving the interface a sense of craft and polish that builds trust

Rive & Lottie Animations

I use Rive for interactive, state-based animations that respond to user input in real time, toggle switches, loading states, onboarding illustrations. For simpler, playback-style animations, Lottie provides lightweight delivery that performs well across platforms.

This expertise is what Rodney Arauz, Head of Software Solutions at RednBlue, highlighted: "Norman has outstanding expertise in animation using tools like Rive and Lottie. His ability to bring interfaces to life with micro-interactions is something rare to find."

Branded Motion Graphics

Beyond interfaces, I've created motion graphics for RednBlue's marketing and brand communications, animated logos, explainer sequences, social media content, and event visuals. These pieces balance brand consistency with creative storytelling, using motion to make the brand feel dynamic and alive.
