App Concept

Relay for Life

UX redesign of the FANLYC platform, enhancing digital participation for a cancer awareness initiative through refined task flows, social features, and a cohesive UI system.

Role

UX/UI Designer

Client

FANLYC (Relay for Life Panama)

Type

App Concept / Redesign

Platform

Mobile (iOS & Android)

Overview

Relay for Life is a global cancer awareness and fundraising event organized by the American Cancer Society. In Panama, FANLYC (Fundacion Nacional de Lucha Contra el Cancer) coordinates the initiative, engaging communities in overnight relay events, fundraising campaigns, and survivor celebrations.

The existing digital experience wasn't keeping pace with participant expectations. I redesigned the app concept to improve accessibility, engagement, and the overall experience of participating in Relay for Life events digitally.

The Challenge

Participants needed a better way to engage with Relay for Life events before, during, and after. The existing platform had fragmented task flows, limited social features, and an inconsistent visual language that made it harder for both new and returning participants to navigate.

Key problems to solve:

  • Onboarding was confusing for first-time participants unfamiliar with Relay for Life
  • Event registration and team management flows were multi-step with unclear progress
  • No meaningful social layer to connect participants and build community momentum
  • Inconsistent UI patterns across different sections of the app

Approach

  • Task flow audit: Mapped every existing user flow, identifying friction points and drop-off moments in registration, team creation, and donation flows
  • Simplified registration: Reduced the event sign-up process from 7 steps to 3, with clear progress indicators and smart defaults
  • Social features: Added team activity feeds, milestone celebrations, and participant leaderboards to build community engagement
  • Cohesive UI system: Built a consistent component library with clear hierarchy, accessible colors, and reusable patterns across the entire app

Key Design Decisions

Event-Centric Navigation

Restructured the app around the event lifecycle: pre-event preparation, live event participation, and post-event celebration. Each phase surfaces different content and actions, so participants always know what's relevant right now.

Community & Social Layer

Added team activity feeds, fundraising milestones with celebratory animations, and participant stories. The goal was to make digital participation feel as connected and emotional as being at a physical relay event.

Accessible Design

Given the diverse participant base, from young students to cancer survivors and their families, accessibility was central. High-contrast text, clear iconography, large touch targets, and straightforward language throughout.

Outcome

Delivered a complete app concept with refined task flows, a social engagement layer, and a unified UI system. The redesign addressed the core usability problems while adding meaningful community features that support the emotional mission of Relay for Life.
